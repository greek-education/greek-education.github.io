Interesting facts

Age

The Greek language has a history of at least 3,500 years. The earliest evidence of Greek comes from Linear B tablets (14th–13th century BCE). It is also the oldest recorded language in Europe that is still in use today.

Influence

Many words in European languages have Greek roots. For example: Philosophy: from “φίλος” (love) and “σοφία” (wisdom). Democracy: from “δήμος” (people) and “κράτος” (power). Most scientific and medical terminology is based on Greek.

Alphabet

The Greek alphabet is the oldest alphabet in continuous use. It introduced vowels into writing systems, making it easier to read compared to consonantal scripts like Phoenician.

Evolution

Although it evolves, Greek retains many words and structures recognizable from Ancient Greek. An educated Greek speaker can understand significant parts of ancient texts, such as Homer’s Iliad or Odyssey.

Where can you find it

Greek is the official language in Greece and Cyprus and one of the official languages of the European Union. It is spoken by approximately 13–14 million people worldwide, with communities in the USA, Australia, and Europe.

Uniquenes

It contains unique words that are hard to translate. For example: Filotimo -> Describes a sense of honor, integrity, and selflessness. Meraki -> The passion and love someone puts into what they do. Meraki -> The passion and love someone puts into what they do.
